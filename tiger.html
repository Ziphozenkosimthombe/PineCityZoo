PANDA

The Tiger (Panthera tigris) is the largest of all big cats and one of nature’s most awe-inspiring predators. With its striking orange coat, black stripes (unique as fingerprints!), and powerful build, this solitary hunter reigns supreme across Asia’s forests, grasslands, and mangroves. Though once widespread, tigers now survive in only 6% of their historic range, with fewer than 5,000 left in the wild.

✔ Size & Strength: Males weigh 180-300 kg (400-660 lbs) and can leap 6-9 meters (20-30 feet) in a single bound. ✔ Stealth Hunter: Stripes provide camouflage, while padded paws allow silent stalking. ✔ Swimming Skills: Unlike most cats, tigers love water and are strong swimmers. ✔ Roar & Communication: Their deep roars can be heard 3 km (1.8 miles) away, used to mark territory.

Tigers are apex predators, hunting deer, wild boar, and even crocodiles. A single tiger needs 50-60 large prey animals per year, requiring vast territories. Sadly, poaching (for skins/bone trade) and habitat loss have pushed tigers to near extinction, with three subspecies already gone forever.
