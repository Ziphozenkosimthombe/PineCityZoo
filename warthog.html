WARTHOG

The Warthog (Phacochoerus africanus) is a wild member of the pig family, famous for its tough, wrinkly skin, curved tusks, and comical sprint with its tail held high like a flag. Native to the grasslands, savannas, and woodlands of sub-Saharan Africa, these resilient animals thrive in harsh environments where many others struggle.

Warthogs are omnivorous, using their snouts to dig for roots, bulbs, and even small animals. They rely on wallows to cool off and mud baths to protect against sunburn and parasites.
