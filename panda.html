PANDA

The Giant Panda (Ailuropoda melanoleuca) is one of the world’s most beloved and iconic bears, instantly recognizable by its black-and-white fur and gentle demeanor. Native to the mountainous bamboo forests of central China, pandas are a national treasure and a global symbol of wildlife conservation. Despite their classification as bears, pandas have a unique diet consisting 99% of bamboo, consuming up to 12-38 kg (26-84 lbs) daily to meet their energy needs.

Adult pandas typically weigh 70-160 kg (150-350 lbs), with males being larger than females. They are mostly solitary animals, communicating through scent markings and occasional vocalizations. Females are fertile for just 2-3 days per year, making reproduction challenging—a key reason for their endangered status.

Thanks to intense conservation efforts, including breeding programs and habitat protection, wild panda numbers have slowly risen. However, they remain threatened by habitat fragmentation and climate change (which affects bamboo growth).
