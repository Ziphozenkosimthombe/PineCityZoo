GEMSBOK

The Gemsbok (Oryx gazella) is a striking antelope species belonging to the family Bovidae. Known for its elegant, long horns and distinctive black-and-white facial markings, the Gemsbok is a true icon of the African desert and savannah. Both males and females possess these impressive horns, which can grow up to 85 cm (33 inches) in length.

Gemsbok are well-adapted to arid environments, such as the Kalahari and Namib Deserts, where they can survive without water for long periods by extracting moisture from the plants they eat. Their pale, grayish-brown coat reflects sunlight, helping them stay cool, while their black markings reduce glare and enhance camouflage.
