MONKEY

The Monkey is a highly intelligent and agile primate belonging to the suborder Haplorhini, with over 260 known species found across tropical and subtropical regions worldwide. Monkeys are divided into two main groups: Old World monkeys (Africa and Asia) with narrow noses and non-prehensile tails, and New World monkeys (Americas) with flat noses and often long, grasping tails. These social creatures display remarkable adaptability, living in diverse habitats from rainforests to savannas and even urban areas.

Monkeys vary dramatically in size, from the tiny Pygmy Marmoset (just 100 grams) to the large Mandrill (weighing up to 50 kg). Most species are omnivorous, eating fruits, leaves, insects, and occasionally small animals. Their dexterous hands and opposable thumbs allow them to skillfully manipulate objects, use tools, and even exhibit problem-solving abilities rivaling great apes.

Living in complex social hierarchies, monkeys communicate through vocalizations, facial expressions, and body language. Some species like Japanese Macaques are known for unique cultural behaviors, such as bathing in hot springs. Unfortunately, many monkey species face threats from habitat destruction, hunting, and the illegal pet trade, with some critically endangered.
