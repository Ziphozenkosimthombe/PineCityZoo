LION

The Lion (Panthera leo), often called the "King of the Jungle," is one of the most iconic big cats and a symbol of strength and courage. Unlike other cats, lions are highly social, living in groups called prides that consist of related females, their cubs, and a coalition of males. These majestic predators once roamed across Africa, Europe, and Asia, but today, they are primarily found in sub-Saharan Africa, with a small population in India's Gir Forest.

Male lions are easily recognized by their impressive manes, which range from blond to dark brown and signal dominance and health. They can weigh up to 190 kg (420 lbs), while lionesses, the primary hunters, are slightly smaller but more agile. Lions are apex predators, preying on animals like zebras, wildebeests, and buffalo, often hunting cooperatively at night. Their powerful roars can be heard up to 8 km (5 miles) away, serving to mark territory and communicate with the pride.
