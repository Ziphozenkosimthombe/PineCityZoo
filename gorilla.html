GORILLA

The Gorilla (Gorilla gorilla and Gorilla beringei) is the largest of all living primates, sharing 98.3% of their DNA with humans. These powerful yet gentle giants belong to the Hominidae family, which also includes chimpanzees, orangutans, and humans. There are two species—the Western Gorilla (Gorilla gorilla) and the Eastern Gorilla (Gorilla beringei)—each with distinct subspecies, such as the Mountain Gorilla and Western Lowland Gorilla.

Gorillas are known for their immense strength, with males (called silverbacks) weighing up to 200 kg (440 lbs) and standing 1.7 meters (5.5 feet) tall when upright. Despite their size, they are primarily herbivorous, feeding on leaves, stems, fruit, and occasionally insects. Their knuckle-walking locomotion and strong social bonds make them fascinating to observe.

Gorillas live in tight-knit family groups led by a dominant silverback, who protects and guides the troop. They communicate through vocalizations, body language, and facial expressions, displaying complex emotions similar to humans. Sadly, gorillas are critically endangered due to habitat loss, poaching, and disease.
